Typography Experiment

The goal of this experiment is to compare side by side different combinations of fonts for display (headers) and copy (main text).

The text below comes from the start of the FAQ page, which features a mixture of different levels of headers and copy including list items and links. Note that extra p elements, not present in the original markup, have been inserted to delineate paragraphs within dd elements more clearly.

The style of the fonts selected should be considered in relation with the meaning of the text displayed, in the specific context of the service.

Frequently Asked Questions

General

What is Bountysource?

Bountysource is the funding platform for open-source software. Users can improve the open-source projects they love by creating/collecting bounties and pledging to fundraisers.

What types of projects are allowed on Bountysource?

Any type of Open-Source or Free Software (as in speech) projects are allowed. Generally speaking, any software licenses approved by either the Open Source Initiative or the Free Software Foundation are acceptable.

For a full list of OSI-approved licenses, please see http://opensource.org/licenses/alphabetical.

For a full list of GNU licenses, please see http://www.gnu.org/licenses/.

You mentioned Free Software. How is that different from Open-Source?

Free Software and Open-Source software are, generally speaking, two sides of the same coin. Both can be considered separate movements within the same community, but with varying goals. We suggest you read up on both sides yourself to better understand them.

  1. "Free Software": http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/open-source-misses-the-point.html
  2. "Open-Source": http://opensource.org/faq#free-software
